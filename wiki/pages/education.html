Card game

To present the idea of biology more amusingly, our team created a fun game inspired by Uno called “Bio-Synth: A Genomic Conquest”.

"BioSynth: Genomic Conquest" is a strategy board game that immerses players in genetics and genomics. Participants in this competitive game strategically build genetic sequences on their creatures using DNA Sequence Cards (A, T, C, G). The gameplay becomes even more dynamic with adding Power Cards such as Mutation and Catalyst cards. Players roll dice to receive DNA Sequence Cards, which they then use to create legitimate genetic sequences. The race to genomic dominance is to be the first to finish three genomic sequences. This game entertains and educates players about genetics and sequencing engagingly and interactively, making it an excellent learning method.

The game's components, which include the game board, DNA Sequence Cards, Organism Tokens, and Power Cards, provide players with an immersive experience. Including Catalyst and Mutation cards in the game adds a layer of strategy and unpredictability, guaranteeing that each round presents a new and exciting challenge. BioSynth offers an enjoyable and instructive experience, whether you're a genetic enthusiast or just searching for a unique and intriguing board game.

Bioethics group discussion

A thought-provoking Bio-Ethics Group Discussion was organised at Tryst, the campus tech fest, to involve both students and staff in debates about bioethical challenges connected to synthetic biology. This event's selection procedure was exceedingly competitive, with participants originally needing to submit essays analysing bioethics in response to a particular question. The evaluation panel, lead by Prof Amit Das of IIT Delhi, assessed critical thinking capabilities, topic comprehension, and communication talents among 15 individuals chosen from a large number of submissions.

The group discussion was divided into two rounds, each focusing on a different aspect of bioethics. The first round of discussion focused on the ethical implications of gene editing for environmental conservation, including possible hazards and benefits. The second round delves into the thorny subject of designer babies, investigating the societal repercussions and ethical constraints of genetically manipulating embryos. The event sought to promote not just critical thought on the ethical elements of biotechnological advances, but also a better grasp of bioethics, fostering ethical reasoning and responsible behaviour in the field of biotechnology. This effort emphasises the significance of incorporating bioethics into research in order to create and preserve public confidence in genetic engineering, as well as the need to evaluate the societal worth and future repercussions of scientific progress.

Poster:

Our team at IIT Delhi has developed an impactful poster targeting the general public, especially our student community, to raise awareness about heavy metal toxicity. Strategically placed in key locations, the poster contains a QR code that leads to our detailed pamphlet, accessible here. This resource delves into the dangers of heavy metal poisoning caused by metals like lead, mercury, and arsenic found in everyday elements such as air, water, and consumer products. Exposure can result from various sources, including contaminated seafood, lead pipes, and certain medications. The health consequences are severe, ranging from fatigue and headaches to severe disorders like cancer and neurological issues. Early detection through medical tests is crucial for appropriate treatment. Our initiative aims to equip the community with knowledge to act proactively against heavy metal exposure.
